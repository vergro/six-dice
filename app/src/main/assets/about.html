Six Dice Game Rules

Objective of the Game

Rules for Six Dice are similar to Yahtzee or Yacht except there are 6 dice instead of the usual 5, and 4 rolls instead of 3.

The game consists of 16 (or 32) rounds. In each round, you roll the dice and then score the roll in one of 16 categories. The object of the game is to maximize your total score.

You must score once in each category -- which means that towards the end of the game you may have to settle for scoring zero in some categories. The score is determined by a different rule for each category; see the section on Scoring below.

The game ends once all 16 categories have been scored.

Rolling the Dice

You have six dice which you can roll, represented by the die faces at the bottom of the screen. To start with, you roll all dice by clicking on the Roll button. After you roll all dice, you can either score the current roll, or re-roll any or all of the six dice.

To re-roll some of the dice, click on the die face to toggle the die to the "saved" position (marked with a green light), then click on the Re-roll button. This will re-roll the unselected dice, leaving the selected ones unchanged.

You can roll the dice a total of four times -- the initial roll (in which you must roll all the dice), plus three re-rolls of any or all dice. After rolling four times, you must score the roll.

Once you've scored the roll, you roll all the dice again and repeat the process. You continue until all 16 categories have been filled, at which time the game is over.

Scoring

Once you have the dice face combination you want to score, you score the roll in one of the 16 categories. You do this by clicking on one of the white boxes in either the right or left columns. Once a category has been scored, it is closed out for the rest of the game; you can change a category’s score as long as it is green.  After you click on the “Roll” button for the next turn the category will turn yellow and can no longer be changed.

Left Scores (Total A)

3 Pair

For 3 Pair you must have 3 pairs of dice.  3 Pair is worth either 25 points, or the sum of the dice, whichever is more.

2 Triples

For 2 Triples you must have 2 Three-of-a-kinds.  2 Triples is worth either 25 points, or the sum of the dice, whichever is more.

4 & 5 of a Kind

For 4 of a Kind, you must have at least four of the same die faces. If so, you total all the die faces and score that total. Similarly for 5 of a Kind, except that you must have 5 of the 6 die faces the same. So for example, if you rolled:

Full House

Full House is when you have both 3 of a kind, and a Pair, and is always worth 35 points.

Chance

This is the extra category where anything goes.  You simply add the total of all the dice and put the total in this box.

6 of a Kind

6 of a Kind is a Yacht! and is worth 60 points.  If you roll more than one Yacht in a single game, you will earn a 100 point bonus for each additional Yacht you roll, provided that you have already scored a 60 in the Yacht category. If you have not scored in the Yacht category, you will not receive a bonus. If you have scored a zero in the Yacht category, you cannot receive any bonuses during the current game.  To earn the bonus 100 points, simply score in a different category (Left or Right) and the bonus points will automatically be added to your total.

Settings

“Reset High Scores” Clicking on this button will reset all of the high scores for the game back to zero, and once done cannot be undone.

Double Mode

Rules for Double Mode are identical to Single Mode except now there are two columns for the Right side, and two columns for the Left side.  This gives the player two chances to use each category.  The player is given 32 turns instead of 16.  The player must score both Yacht’s before the 100 point extra Yacht bonus is awarded. 

Sequential Mode

Rules for Sequential Mode are similar to Double Mode except for the items “Right” or “B” category must be completed sequentially.  For example once the “Ones” category has been scored in, it unlocks the “Twos” category for that column.  This added twist adds an extra layer of difficulty to the game.

Bonus Roll Mode

Rules for Bonus Roll Mode are similar to Double Mode  except there are 10 “Bonus Rolls” that can be used as a 5th or extra roll.  After the player has rolled their final roll, they have the option of using one of the “Bonus Rolls”, and this can be done a total of 10 times in one game.

Online Scores

When a game is completed a player can select “Submit Score” upload their high score to Google Play Games.  These Leaderboards can be viewed on the Play menu by selecting “Leaderboards”.   There is a separate Leaderboard for Double Mode, Original Mode, Sequential Mode, and Bonus Roll. 

Achievements

Achievements have been added to Six Dice and will automatically be tracked and scored for the player.  To view the status of completed achievements click on the “Achievements” button on the Play menu. You can earn XP by completing Achievements.
